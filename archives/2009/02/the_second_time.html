THE SECOND TIME AROUND - continued

Prologue:  As I'm sure you all know, Bernie passed away on February 2nd, but this will be written as though she is still here, because at the time, she was.

ZION NATIONAL PARK in south-west Utah, is the most visited of the "big 5" we explored on this trip in mid-November.  Visited by over 2 million people annually, the park has instituted a bus service, which runs every 10 minutes from about 6:00AM to 10:00 PM because there is just nowhere to park that many visitors' vehicles.  It is free and goes to all the famous points of interest.  Again here, we met up with Jim and Carolyn from New England and although Jim's back was giving him some grief, we were able to see all the sights.

This shot, taken at  the Weeping Wall, is the story of being in the right place at the right time. Ordinary evergreens back lighted by the sun, created this unique image.  The Weeping Wall itself was kind of a disappointment, although we were told that earlier in the season there is more water coming out through the rocks and the effect is much better.
